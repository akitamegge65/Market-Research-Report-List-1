<p><p>The Internet of Nano Things (IoNT) can be categorized into two primary market types based on communication distance: short-distance and long-distance communication. Short-distance communication typically involves technologies like Bluetooth and Zigbee, enabling efficient data exchange among nearby nano devices within a limited range. In contrast, long-distance communication utilizes cellular networks, satellite links, or LoRa, allowing for broader coverage and connectivity across significant distances. These distinctions are crucial for applications in healthcare, environmental monitoring, and smart cities, where diverse operational requirements exist.</p></p>
