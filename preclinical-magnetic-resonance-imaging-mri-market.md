<p><p>The preclinical MRI market encompasses both structural and functional imaging types. Structural imaging focuses on providing detailed anatomical information about tissues and organs, aiding in the assessment of disease progression and treatment effects. In contrast, functional imaging evaluates physiological processes, such as blood flow and metabolic activity, offering insights into the functionality of biological systems. Together, these imaging modalities enhance research capabilities in drug development and disease modeling, supporting advancements in preclinical studies and therapeutic evaluations.</p></p>
