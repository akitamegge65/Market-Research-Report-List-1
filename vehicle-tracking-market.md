<p><p>The vehicle tracking market primarily consists of two types: wired and wireless tracking. Wired tracking involves installing a GPS device directly into the vehicle's electrical system, providing consistent power and reliability but requiring professional installation. Wireless tracking, on the other hand, employs GPS technology to transmit location data via cellular networks or satellite, offering flexibility and ease of installation. Both types cater to various needs, including fleet management, personal security, and theft prevention, enhancing overall vehicle monitoring and control.</p></p>
