<p><p>The ADHD therapeutics market is primarily divided into two categories: stimulants and non-stimulants. Stimulants, such as methylphenidate and amphetamines, are the most commonly prescribed and work by enhancing dopamine and norepinephrine levels in the brain, improving attention and focus. Non-stimulants, including atomoxetine and guanfacine, offer alternative treatment options for individuals who may not respond well to stimulants or have specific health concerns. Both types aim to alleviate ADHD symptoms, but they operate through different mechanisms and have distinct profiles in terms of side effects and efficacy.</p></p>
